About Me

My name is Vuk Radovic and I come from the capital of Serbia – Belgrade. Entering year 8 of elementary school, I decided to fully commit myself to studying maths, physics and related fields. Ten years later, I hold a Masters in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics from Oxford and work as a quantitative researcher. Since graduating about two years ago, I have been more and more streamlined into my primary occupation and thus, the threads connecting me to other topics I am passionate about are getting thinner. Therefore, I believe that starting this blog will help me achieve the desired consistency, focus and will improve the quality of things I do that do not have a direct measurable output.

Chasing Virtue

The information flow into one’s brain has never been greater. Dopamine blasts coming from social media, video games, alchol, drugs and other forms of addiction came at a very high cost. We swapped hours of hard work with a huge future reward for instant pleasures that fade away as quickly as they arrive. Unfortunately, being aware of this is simply not enough for going back to the path of acquiring life worthy skills and achieving what the Ancient Greeks called eudemonia – a true internal happiness. The idea of this blog is to create a testimony of me trying to do exactly that.

Keeping Focus

It is not uncommon that we understand things better after explaining or writing. After all, having to sit down, think and concisely write about something definitely helps solidifying the knowledge. Therefore, by forcing myself to write about ideas I find interesting will both improve my consistency and push me an extra mile towards better understanding the topic.

Topics

Having somewhat justified my motivation, I will mainly be sharing my thoughts on maths, physics, machine learning and other technical fields. However, I will not hesitate to explore areas I am enthusiastic about but not very proficient in. These include but are not limited to philosophy, music, writing and learning new languages.
